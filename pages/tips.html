Tips & tricks

Fix small problems before they get big

Quick guides for the issues we see most — and when it's time to bring it in.

Overheating laptop?

Clean the vents with compressed air every few months and avoid using it on soft surfaces like beds or couches — they block airflow and trap heat.

Battery draining fast?

Check for background apps eating power in Task Manager, lower screen brightness, and keep charge cycles between 20–80% for long-term battery health.

Wi-Fi keeps dropping?

Restart your router monthly, keep it away from thick walls and microwaves, and update your network adapter driver if disconnects are frequent.

Blue screen errors?

Note the stop code shown on screen, then check if it appeared right after a driver or Windows update — rolling that back resolves most sudden BSOD issues.

Spilled liquid on your laptop?

Power it off immediately, unplug it, flip it upside down to drain, and don't turn it back on — bring it in within 24 hours for the best chance of a full recovery.

Fans running loud all the time?

Dust buildup makes fans work harder and louder. A compressed-air cleaning every few months and a thermal repaste every couple of years keeps things quiet and cool.

Troubleshooting guides

Common tech issues, solved

Try these first — if the problem sticks around, our technicians are one message away.

Computer won't turn on

Check the power cable and outlet first, then hold the power button for 10 seconds to discharge residual power, and try again. If nothing happens, it may be a PSU or motherboard issue.

Running painfully slow

Check startup programs, clear temporary files, and confirm you still have at least 15% free disk space. An aging HDD is often the real culprit — an SSD upgrade fixes this fast.

Pop-ups won't stop

Run a full scan with your antivirus, check installed browser extensions for anything unfamiliar, and reset your browser settings if the pop-ups persist.

USB device not detected

Try a different port and cable first. Then check Device Manager for a yellow warning icon — a driver reinstall usually resolves detection issues.

Screen flickering

Update your graphics driver first. If flickering continues on an external monitor, swap the cable — if it's a laptop screen, it may point to a loose display connector.

Printer won't connect

Restart both the printer and router, confirm both are on the same network, and reinstall the latest printer driver from the manufacturer's site.

No sound from speakers

Check the correct output device is selected in sound settings, confirm nothing is muted, and update or reinstall the audio driver if it still won't play.

Strange clicking or grinding noises

Back up your data immediately — a clicking hard drive is often failing. Power down and have it inspected before you lose files, rather than after.

Keyboard keys not responding

Clean under the keys with compressed air first, then check for stuck keys in an online key-tester tool. If only specific keys fail, it's usually a worn switch, not the whole board.

Laptop won't charge

Try a different outlet and cable to rule those out, check the charging port for lint or damage, and confirm the charging light turns on when plugged in.

Apps keep crashing or freezing

Update the app and your OS first, then check Task Manager for high memory or CPU use. Reinstalling the app usually resolves crashes tied to corrupted files.
